Together let’s dive into the world of swimming

Beginner

  • Comfortable entering and exiting the pool independently.
  • Confident floating on front and back for at least 10 seconds.
  • Gliding for a short distance (5-10 feet) with face in the water.
  • Basic flutter kick on front and back.
  • Demonstrates basic arm movements for freestyle and backstroke.
  • Can submerge head and exhale underwater comfortably.

Intermediate

  • Can swim 15-25 meters continuously using freestyle and backstroke.
  • Demonstrates proper breathing technique (side breathing in freestyle).
  • Can tread water for at least 30 seconds.
  • Introduced to breaststroke and basic butterfly kick.
  • Performs a streamlined push-off and kick from the pool wall.
  • Can retrieve objects from deeper water (3-5 feet).

Advanced

  • Swims 50+ meters continuously in freestyle, backstroke, and breaststroke.
  • Executes butterfly stroke with proper timing and technique.
  • Treads water for 2+ minutes using efficient techniques.
  • Performs flip turns and competitive starts.
  • Demonstrates speed and endurance in timed swims.
  • Can perform basic rescue techniques, like assisting another swimmer.

Beginner

Proper Breathing Technique

  • Step 1: Start by standing in shallow water or holding onto the pool edge.
  • Step 2: Take a deep breath and submerge your face into the water.
  • Step 3: Exhale gently underwater through your nose. Practice slowly exhaling in bubbles.
  • Step 4: Raise your head to the side to inhale quickly, ensuring you’re not holding your breath underwater.
  • Step 5: Repeat and get comfortable with this rhythm, practicing in different positions like floating or kicking.

Floating and Buoyancy

  • Step 1: Start by holding onto the poolside with your hands and let your body float freely in the water.
  • Step 2: Keep your body horizontal and try to keep your head above water with your face looking straight up.
  • Step 3: Relax your body and ensure your head is aligned with your spine, avoid arching your back.
  • Step 4: Gradually practice floating without holding onto the side, using gentle arm movements if necessary for stability.

Basic Arm Movements (Freestyle & Backstroke)

  • Step 1: For freestyle, extend one arm in front while the other arm is by your side.
  • Step 2: Pull the extended arm down along your side and back, keeping a relaxed but firm motion.
  • Step 3: Rotate your body slightly with each stroke and switch arms as one arm pushes through the water.
  • Step 4: For backstroke, keep your arms alternating overhead with a smooth, continuous motion while kicking gently.

Intermediate

Streamlined Body Position

  • Step 1: Keep your body horizontal and streamlined in the water. Avoid lifting your head too high.
  • Step 2: Engage your core muscles to maintain a flat and straight position.
  • Step 3: When swimming freestyle, maintain your head in a neutral position, facing down. This minimizes drag.
  • Step 4: Focus on keeping your body aligned throughout the stroke to reduce resistance in the water.

Efficient Kicking

  • Step 1: Practice flutter kicking by keeping your legs straight with a slight bend at the knee.
  • Step 2: Kick from your hips rather than from the knees. Quick, small movements will propel you forward more effectively.
  • Step 3: Keep your ankles loose, and point your toes to reduce drag.
  • Step 4: Gradually increase your kick frequency while maintaining control and consistency.

Breathing Techniques (Bilateral Breathing)

  • Step 1: Practice breathing to both sides (bilateral) for balance. Alternate between turning your head left and right to breathe.
  • Step 2: Inhale quickly through your mouth when you turn your head, and exhale underwater as you return your face to the water.
  • Step 3: Use a rhythmic breathing pattern like “2 strokes – breathe – 2 strokes – breathe.”
  • Step 4: Focus on keeping your stroke consistent while practicing this balanced breathing.

Advanced

Advanced Stroke Techniques (Butterfly and Breaststroke)

  • Step 1: For the butterfly, start with both arms moving simultaneously, pulling in a “keyhole” pattern in the water.
  • Step 2: Keep your body undulating with the arm strokes, using your core to generate power.
  • Step 3: For breaststroke, keep your arms in a circular motion, bringing them in front of you and pushing outward like a frog kick.
  • Step 4: Focus on timing the arm strokes and leg kicks together for smooth, efficient movement.

Flip Turns

  • Step 1: As you approach the wall, start your last stroke and then tuck your body into a ball, bringing your knees to your chest.
  • Step 2: Rotate your body quickly into a somersault, keeping your head tucked.
  • Step 3: Push off the wall with your feet, using a streamlined position to minimize drag.
  • Step 4: Begin your next stroke immediately as you leave the wall.

Endurance Training

  • Step 1: Start by swimming longer distances at a moderate pace.
  • Step 2: Focus on maintaining consistent breathing patterns, and gradually increase your lap count each session.
  • Step 3: Introduce interval training – alternate between high-intensity sprints and recovery swims.
  • Step 4: Incorporate different strokes to build overall endurance.
